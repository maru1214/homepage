About

Hello, my name is Yusei Fujimaru.
I am a first-year PhD student in the Department of Astronomy at Kyoto University.
My research focuses on Type Ia supernova remnants, or SNRs, using three-dimensional hydrodynamical simulations, non-equilibrium ionization calculations, and thermal X-ray spectral modeling.
Type Ia supernova remnants provide important clues to the nature of supernova explosions and their surrounding environments. Previous studies have often used one-dimensional models to investigate the effects of explosion models and ambient environments. However, real supernova remnants show large-scale asymmetries and small-scale complex structures, indicating that three-dimensional effects are essential for understanding their evolution and observational properties.
Recently, high-resolution X-ray spectroscopy with the XRISM satellite has made it possible to study detailed line structures, plasma conditions, and elemental abundances in SNRs. To interpret these observations, realistic three-dimensional simulations and theoretical calculations of high-resolution X-ray spectra are becoming increasingly important.
In my research, I combine three-dimensional hydrodynamical simulations with non-equilibrium ionization calculations to investigate the connection between the three-dimensional structure of Type Ia supernova remnants and their X-ray observational features. Ultimately, I aim to constrain the explosion models and surrounding environments of Type Ia supernova remnants.
